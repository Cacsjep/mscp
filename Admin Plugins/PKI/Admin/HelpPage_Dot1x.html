Client certificates for IEEE 802.1X port authentication
EAP-TLS / device identity

What lives here

802.1X is a network-port authentication protocol used by managed switches to decide whether a device may sit on the wire. Cameras, NVRs, and recording servers each present a client certificate as their identity; this folder is where you issue those client certs.

The cert ends up loaded onto the device (or into the Windows Personal store if it's a server) and the matching Root / Intermediate must also be trusted by the RADIUS server that does the authentication.

Required fields

  • Sign with - a CA already trusted by your RADIUS / NPS server.
  • Display name - identifies the device or user.
  • Common Name - e.g. camera-01.example.local or alice@example.com. Many RADIUS policies key on this value.
  • Validity - commonly 1-2 years. Schedule renewals before expiry; a stale 802.1X cert means devices stop authenticating onto the network.
  • Key algorithm - check what your endpoint device actually accepts. Many older cameras require RSA.

Deployment

For Windows hosts, the PKI Cert Installer drops the cert into the Personal store and ACLs the key for the chosen account. For cameras and other embedded devices, export a PFX from this vault (Export > PFX (cert + key)...) and upload via the device's web UI.
