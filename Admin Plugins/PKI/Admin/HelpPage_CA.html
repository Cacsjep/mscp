Container for the certificate authorities that anchor your internal trust
Two sub-folders inside

What lives here

This is a grouping node, not a folder you place certs in directly. It contains the two CA folders below.

Sub-folderPurpose
Root CertificatesSelf-signed top-of-trust CAs. Sign Intermediates and (optionally) leaf certs.
Intermediate CertificatesCAs signed by a Root, used as the day-to-day issuer for HTTPS / Service / 802.1X leaf certs.

Recommended structure

  1. Issue one Root with a long validity (10 to 20 years) and keep its private key around for emergencies only.
  2. Issue one or more Intermediate CAs (5 to 10 years) and use those for everything in the Client Certificates branch.
  3. Distribute the Root to the Windows Trusted Root Certification Authorities store on every machine that needs to validate the chain. The PKI Cert Installer EXE handles this routing automatically.

That two-level chain lets you rotate the Intermediate without redistributing the Root, and limits blast radius if a key is ever lost.

Quick start

Skip the manual work and click Auto setup on the Overview node: it issues a self-signed Root for 15 years and one Service certificate per registered XProtect™ service signed by that Root, stamped with the current year and month so re-runs sort cleanly.
